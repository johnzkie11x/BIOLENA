Plagiarism

What is Plagiarism?

Plagiarism is the act of using someone else's work, ideas, or intellectual property without proper acknowledgment or permission, presenting it as one's own. This can include copying text, images, or data, as well as paraphrasing someone’s ideas without giving credit. Plagiarism is considered unethical and dishonest in academic, professional, and creative contexts. It can apply to a wide range of materials, including written content, scientific research, art, music, and even software code. The key element of plagiarism is the lack of proper citation or attribution, leading to a misrepresentation of authorship.

Report Cases of Plagiarism.

University of the Philippines (UP) Students – 2019 Plagiarism: In 2019, two students from the University of the Philippines (UP) were involved in a plagiarism controversy after their thesis papers were found to have copied significant portions of content from published research articles and online sources without proper attribution. Consequences: The university investigated the matter, and the students faced academic sanctions. This case emphasized the importance of academic integrity at one of the country's leading institutions and prompted UP to reinforce its policies regarding plagiarism. Learn more

Nature of Punishment.

The nature of the punishment for plagiarism is multifaceted, reflecting the seriousness with which academic institutions, professional organizations, and legal systems view intellectual dishonesty. In educational settings, the immediate consequences often include failure on the assignment or course, with potential for academic probation or even expulsion, depending on the severity of the offense. Beyond academia, plagiarism can lead to significant professional repercussions, such as damage to one's reputation, loss of job opportunities, and legal consequences, including lawsuits or fines, particularly if the infringement involves copyrighted material. These punitive measures are designed not only to penalize the individual but also to uphold the integrity of intellectual work and encourage respect for original authorship.

What is Computer Virus?

A computer virus is a type of malicious software that replicates itself and spreads from one computer to another, often causing harm by corrupting or deleting files, stealing sensitive information, slowing down system performance, or allowing unauthorized access to the computer.

What is Malware Spam?

Malware spam refers to unwanted emails that contain malicious software or links to infected websites, aiming to trick recipients into opening attachments or clicking links. This can lead to malware infections, such as viruses or ransomware, stealing data, installing backdoors for hackers, or spreading the malware to others.

What is Antivirus?

Software designed to detect, prevent, and remove malicious software (malware), such as viruses, worms, and Trojans, from a computer or network. It works by scanning files, programs, and websites for known threats, blocking suspicious activities, and offering real-time protection against potential infections. Antivirus programs often include features like automatic updates, malware removal, and system scanning to help keep devices secure.
